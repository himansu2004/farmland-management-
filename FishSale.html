Fish Farming

Fish farming, or aquaculture, is characterized by several key features essential for successful operations. It involves cultivating fish in controlled environments such as ponds, tanks, or cages, where water quality management is paramount to ensure optimal conditions for growth. Farmers carefully select fish species based on market demand and environmental suitability, while providing balanced diets to meet their nutritional needs. Disease prevention and treatment measures are implemented to safeguard fish health, while responsible waste management practices minimize environmental impact. Harvesting and processing techniques are employed to gather fish at market size and preserve product quality. Ultimately, fish farming integrates various elements such as feeding, stocking, and market access to promote sustainable production and economic viability within the aquaculture industry.
