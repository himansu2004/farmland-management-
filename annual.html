Annual Farmers

Annual farming involves cultivating crops that complete their lifecycle within a single growing season, typically spanning a year. Farmers plant seeds or seedlings at the start of the growing season, nurturing the crops through various stages of growth until harvest. Common annual crops include grains, vegetables, and legumes. Unlike perennial crops, which have longer lifespans, annual crops are replanted each year. Annual farming practices vary based on factors like climate, soil conditions, and available resources, with the aim of maximizing yield and quality within the confines of a single growing cycle.
